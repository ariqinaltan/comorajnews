A Luminary in West Texas Media

From Lubbock to the broader expanses of Texas, Jay’s voice was a familiar resonance in the media sphere. With nearly a quarter-century in the industry, Leeson showcased an uncanny ability to dissect intricate topics, turning them into relatable narratives for the common Texan. His dedication to transparency, storytelling, and education were evident through his extensive body of work.

A Prairie Populist with a Pen

Dubbing himself a “Prairie Populist”, Leeson’s authenticity and grassroots approach to journalism and advocacy were palpable. He had an innate knack for cartoons, often rendering Texas Tech and Big 12 mascots. These creations bore a striking resemblance to the artistic style of Dirk West, another stalwart in the world of cartooning. The Lubbock Avalanche-Journal, where he was a regular contributor, often featured his artwork, making him a household name in many Texan homes.

Voice of the Airwaves

Beyond print, Leeson’s voice graced the radio channels of AM580. His talk show, “The Other Side of Texas”, garnered widespread acclaim and eventually metamorphosed into a podcast. Additionally, viewers of KAMC were no strangers to his insightful political commentary, which was always delivered with a mix of passion and pragmatism.

Academic Achievements and Entrepreneurial Endeavors

A proud alumnus of Texas Tech, Jay Leeson held a bachelor’s degree in journalism. This foundation undoubtedly played a pivotal role in honing his journalistic prowess. In addition to his undergraduate achievements, he pursued higher theological studies, securing a Master’s of Divinity from the esteemed Asbury Theological Seminary.

Displaying entrepreneurial flair, Leeson launched Leeson Communications, LLC in 2017. This marketing and public relations venture was yet another feather in his cap, showcasing his versatility and vision.

Tributes Pour In

Lubbock Representative, Dustin Burrows, succinctly captured the sentiments of many when he remarked on Leeson’s passing. Highlighting Jay’s contribution to rural Texan communities, state politics, and Texas Tech University, Burrows emphasized the significant void his passing has created. Yet, amidst the palpable sorrow, there’s an underlying acknowledgment of Jay’s lasting legacy, which, as Burrows noted, will “continue to resonate for years to come.”
