Chance the Rapper Net Worth – A Profile of One of the Music Industry’s Most Talented Performers

Chance the Rapper is one of the most talked-about musicians in the business today. The Chicago-born artist has captured the hearts of millions worldwide with his unique blend of hip-hop and gospel, skyrocketing to the top of the charts with hits like “No Problem,” “Angels,” and “Same Drugs.” But how much is Chance worth? What’s his story, and how did he get to where he is today? Read on to discover everything you need to know.

What is Chance the Rapper’s Net Worth and Salary?

As of 2021, Chance the Rapper’s net worth is estimated to be approximately $30 million. It’s worth noting that Chance has always been open about his distaste for the traditional music industry and has rejected the lucrative deals offered to him by major record labels. Instead, he’s chosen to earn his fortune independently, selling his music on platforms like SoundCloud and iTunes. Much of Chance’s wealth has also been earned through merchandise and sponsorship deals, with the artist partnering with companies like Nike, Complex, and H&M.

Early Life and Education

Chance the Rapper was born Chancelor Johnathan Bennett on April 16, 1993, in Chicago, Illinois. He grew up with his mother, Lisa Bennett, who worked for the Illinois Attorney General’s office, and his father, Ken Williams-Bennett, who served as an aide to former Chicago mayor Harold Washington and worked for President Barack Obama’s 2008 campaign. Chance attended Jones College Prep High School in Chicago before going on to study at Harold Washington College, where he pursued a degree in leisure studies.

Career

Chance’s career began to take off in 2010 when he released his debut mixtape, “10 Day,” which was based on a suspension he received during his senior year of high school. The mixtape received widespread critical acclaim and eventually led to Chance signing with the independent label, SaveMoney. He released his second mixtape, “Acid Rap,” in 2013, which featured collaborations with artists like Action Bronson, Ab-Soul, and Childish Gambino. “Acid Rap” became a cultural phenomenon, with Chance earning comparisons to legends like Kanye West and Tupac Shakur.

Chance entered the mainstream with the release of his third mixtape, “Coloring Book,” in 2016. The album was praised for its gospel influences and themes of faith and gratitude, earning Chance three Grammy Awards for Best New Artist, Best Rap Performance, and Best Rap Album. He followed up with his debut studio album, “The Big Day,” in 2019, which featured collaborations with artists like John Legend and Shawn Mendes.

Personal Life

Chance the Rapper is a devout Christian who has been open about the role that faith has played in his life and music. He married his longtime girlfriend, Kirsten Corley, in March 2019, and the couple has two daughters together. Chance has been known for his philanthropic efforts, donating millions to public schools in his hometown of Chicago and founding the SocialWorks charity organization.

Real Estate

In 2019, Chance the Rapper made headlines when he purchased a 35,000 square foot mansion in the suburban Chicago area of Highland Park for $4 million. The mansion boasts features like a nine-car garage, a movie theater, and an indoor basketball court, and the property also includes a separate guesthouse.

Legacy

Chance the Rapper has already cemented his legacy as one of the most innovative and talented musicians of his time. He’s managed to achieve massive success without sacrificing his independence or authenticity and has become a role model for aspiring artists everywhere. With his philanthropic work and dedication to his faith, Chance has also shown that he’s committed to making a positive impact on the world.
