Why do tennis players grunt?

Tennis players grunt because it helps them concentrate, breathe, and increase their intensity. Grunting can be beneficial to every tennis player, as it is scientifically proven to increase shot velocity by roughly 5%.

Why do tennis players make such weird noises?

Scientists have suggested that the pitch of a tennis player's grunt gives some clues as to whether they are going to win or not. Do tennis players really need to grunt?

Louise Deeley, a sports psychologist at Roehampton University, believes that grunting is part of the rhythm for tennis players: "The timing of when they actually grunt helps them with the rhythm of how they're hitting and how they're pacing things".

Does grunting make you better at tennis?

When the impact of a grunt is investigated, there is evidence that hitting performance is enhanced. Skilled university tennis players were found to hit with a 3.8% increase in groundstroke hitting velocity when they grunted. For a serve, a 4.9% enhancement in velocity was found among players who grunted.
