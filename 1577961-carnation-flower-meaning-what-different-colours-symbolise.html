Carnation flower meaning: What do the different colours symbolise?

What do carnations symbolise? Carnations have different symbolisms depending on the colour of the flower. However, basic clove pink symbolism embraces the ideas of fascination, devotion and love.

Naturally, the flowers are pinkish-purple. However, through selective breeding, you can find clove pink in different shades of white, red, yellow, blue and purple, and each colour symbolises something different.

The love aspect of clove pink includes a mother's love or a suitor's love for his bride. That is why these flowers are highly considered when you want to give a bouquet to your partner. When it comes to the devotion aspect of clove pink, many people use these flowers in wedding ceremonies to symbolise devotion. It is a way of showing the couple's devotion to each other.
