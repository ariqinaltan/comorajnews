How Much Is The Tuition Fee In Seattle University Per Year

People are currently reading this guide.

So, how much does a fancy Seattle University education cost? Buckle up, buttercup!

Let's face it, college tuition these days can make your bank account do a backflip into the abyss. But fear not, intrepid knowledge seeker! We're here to crack the code on what it'll cost to become a Seattle University Redhawk (their mascot is a majestic red hawk, by the way, pretty cool).

The Big Reveal: A Tuition Breakdown (with a dash of humor)

Alright, alright, I know you're itching for the nitty-gritty. Here's the skinny:

  • Full-Time Undergrads (12-18 credits per quarter): This is where most folks fall. You're looking at a cool $55,620 per year for tuition alone. Think of it as an investment in your future awesomeness, with a side of student loan debt (but hey, it's all about the journey, right?).
  • Part-Time Warriors (1-11 credits per quarter): Maybe you're working or juggling meteors as a hobby (hey, no judgement here). In this case, you'll pay per credit, with a starting price of $1,236.

Wait, There's More! (Because College Isn't All Tuition)

Now, before you faint dramatically (we've all been there), remember, tuition is just one piece of the puzzle. You'll also need to factor in:

  • Housing: Dorms are swanky but come at a cost. Expect to pay extra for the privilege of having your laundry magically disappear (or maybe that's just wishful thinking).
  • Meal Plans: Unless you're a master ramen chef, you'll probably want a meal plan to avoid living off vending machine snacks (trust me, it gets old fast).
  • Activity Fees: Because who wants a college experience without a sprinkle of clubs, intramural sports, and existential campus lectures, right?

The Bottom Line (with a sprinkle of hope)

Seattle University isn't exactly giving away degrees like free candy, but they do have a generous financial aid program. Over 95% of students receive some form of financial aid, so don't let the sticker shock scare you away.
