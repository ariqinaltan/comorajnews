How Many Airports In Austin Texas

You're Going to Austin, Baby! But How Many Landing Strips Do They Need?

So, you've booked that sweet trip to Austin, Texas. Yeehaw! Live music, breakfast tacos, and maybe even a glimpse of Matthew McConaughey filming something mysterious (hey, a girl can dream). But before you start practicing your two-step, there's a crucial question: Just how many airports does Austin have, anyway?

This, my friend, is where things get a little... interesting. Buckle up, because we're about to untangle the truth behind Austin's aviation situation.

The Big Kahuna: Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (AUS)

This is the big cheese, the main squeeze, the Beyonce of Austin airports. Austin-Bergstrom International (lovingly nicknamed "ABIA" by the locals) is where all the major airlines strut their stuff. Think fancy international connections, bustling terminals, and that delightful pre-flight scramble for the perfect breakfast taco (because, let's be honest, that's what truly matters).

Fun Fact: ABIA is named after a local war hero, Captain John Bergstrom. So next time you're stuck in security, take a moment to appreciate his sacrifice (and maybe silently thank him for all the delicious taco options).

The Little Airstrip That Could: Austin Executive Airport (EDC)

Think of this as the quirky cousin of ABIA. Austin Executive Airport is smaller, cozier, and perfect for private planes, business jets, and the occasional tiny house on wheels that's sprouted wings (hey, it's Austin, weirder things have happened).

Here's the thing: You probably won't be flying into EDC unless you're a high roller or dating a pilot (lucky you!). But it's a fun fact to have in your back pocket, just in case you need to impress someone at a trivia night (because who doesn't love dominating trivia night?).

The Great Austin Airport Conspiracy (Okay, Maybe Not That Dramatic)

Here's where things get a bit fuzzy. Some websites will tell you there are a whopping 27 airports in Austin. Now, hold on to your cowboy hat. That number includes everything from tiny private landing strips to glorified glorified helicopter pads. For most folks flying into Austin, the only two that really matter are ABIA and EDC.

So, there you have it! The truth about Austin's airports, delivered with a side of humor (and maybe a touch of taco obsession). Now you can book your flight with confidence, knowing exactly where your plane will be touching down. Safe travels, and don't forget to bring your dancing shoes (and your appetite for tacos)!
