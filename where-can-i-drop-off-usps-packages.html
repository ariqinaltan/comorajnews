Where Can I Drop Off USPS Packages? (Full Guide)

Are you getting ready to send an important parcel? 

The United States Postal Service is one of the largest mail distribution centers in the country. The company handles all types of mail and ships it quickly and efficiently to domestic and international destinations. While USPS has a number of competitors, the company has managed to remain very popular. One of the reasons for this is the increased number of convenient drop-off locations. 

If you are wondering, ‘where can I drop off USPS packages?’, then it’s time to find out…

The Approved Postal Provider Program

The United States Postal Service has launched a series of services under this special program. This is intended to make shipping parcels easier for customers and provide them with more options. A large percentage of customers work full-time hours during the week, making it difficult to get to a post office branch.

One of the most popular services under the Approved Postal Provider Program is Approved Shipper. This allows certain retailers to offer USPS shipping services. Customers can leave their parcels at the branches of participating banks, grocery stores, and other companies.

It’s all about customer convenience…

This special partnership is beneficial for all the companies involved. For USPS, the main benefit is extra convenience for their customers. The other participating companies benefit from extra foot traffic and a small fee for the service. Many people will also take the opportunity to do some shopping, which increases revenue for those companies.

The ‘Stamps To Go’ Service

Stamps To Go is another popular service that is part of the Approved Postal Provider Program. This provides customers with a wide range of packing and shipping materials. Customers can purchase items from a store and pack and ship them in a single, convenient location.

Where Can I Drop Off USPS Packages? – Drop Off Locations

In the past, the main place to deposit your parcel was at a branch of the United States Postal Service. A large number of popular retailers now receive mail through the Approved Postal Provider Program. Here are twelve different places you can leave your parcel along with details of the available services. 

1 United States Postal Service (USPS) Collection Boxes

These blue boxes can be found in locations that receive a lot of foot traffic. This is a convenient way of sending your parcel if it has been correctly packaged and you have attached the postage. All you need to do is place your parcel in the box, and it will be collected by a mail carrier. You can check the USPS website to find the location of your nearest collection box.

2 Self-Service Kiosks

USPS operates a large number of self-service kiosks that allow you to drop off your parcel. This is a good way of sending your parcel without having to visit a post office branch. Self-service kiosks can usually be found in major cities and other densely populated areas.

To find your nearest self-service kiosk, go to the USPS website. Select self-service kiosks from the dropdown menu. Once you arrive at the kiosk, you can follow the prompts on the screen to successfully ship your parcel.

3 Contract Postal Units

These contracted units serve as independent partners of the United States Postal Service through the Approved Postal Provider Program. Selected retailers are permitted to offer USPS shipping supplies and services. Customers can pay for their parcel to be shopped and have items packaged in one convenient location. 

4 Walmart 

This is one of the largest and most convenient companies that offer the USPS drop-off service. Most Walmart locations in the United States offer this particular service. It is best to enquire about the service at the customer service counter in your nearest store. 

5 Staples

This office supplies company sells everything you need to properly pack your parcel and send it on its way. This is a good option if you have just purchased an item that you want to send right away. Simply purchase the packing materials in the store and secure the contents before choosing the best shipping service.

Many Staples stores also feature the United States Postal Service drop boxes. If your parcel is ready to send, you simply need to pop it into the box. You can also take the opportunity to pick up some office supplies at the same time. 

6 Walgreens

This retail chain boasts more than eight thousand locations across the United States. Many of the locations are open around the clock for the convenience of customers. Sending your parcel via Walgreens is an excellent option if you are planning to do some late-night shopping. 

If your parcel is ready to send, you simply need to place it in the designated drop box in the store. Alternatively, you can ask for assistance at the customer service counter in the store. Walgreens stores also sell packing tape and other materials to make sure your parcel is properly sealed. 

7 CVS

You can send a parcel and collect a prescription at the same time in a CVS store. Simply look for a drop-box bearing the United States Postal Service label and pop your parcel inside. If you need a little extra help, you can go to the customer service counter in the store. 

8 Office Depot

Like Staples, visiting Office Depot is a good option if you also need some office supplies. You can find a whole host of office supplies and other types of items in one convenient location. Many locations offer United States Postal Service drop-boxes to make the process very quick and easy. 

These drop boxes are usually located at the front of the store, so you don’t even have to go inside. If you are driving past on your way to work, simply stop and pop your parcel in the box. You can also take advantage of other postal services at the customer service desk in the Office Depot store. 

9 Safeway

Also known as Albertsons in some parts of the United States, Safeway sells a huge range of groceries at low prices. These grocery stores are used by millions of customers, and Safeway also offers a range of convenient services. This has helped the company to attract more customers by allowing them to take care of various tasks in one place.

Therefore, it should come as no surprise to find that Safeway has partnered with USPS. Most locations boast a special desk or kiosk bearing the USPS logo. If the store does not offer this feature, you can use the provided collection box or go to the customer service desk. 

10 Costco 

If you are a Costco member, you can do some shopping and send your parcel with ease. Most Costco locations throughout the United States participate in the Approved Shipper program. While there are no drop-boxes in these stores, you can send your parcel at the customer service desk. This is designed to be a quick and easy service, and the main benefit is that you will receive a receipt. 

11 Sam’s Club

This national bulk grocery retailer also participates in the United States Postal Service Approved Shipper program. All you need to do is take your parcel to the customer service desk. Your parcel will be processed, and you will be given a receipt, which includes the tracking number. 

12 United Heritage Credit Union

If you need to do some banking, you can send a parcel at the same time. United Heritage Credit Union is the only financial corporation that offers this service. However, most of the company’s branches only sell booklets of stamps. It is best to check in advance if you can send your parcel from your nearest branch.

Where Can I Drop Off USPS Packages? – Final Thoughts

The USPS Approved Postal Provider Program means that customers can drop off their parcels in a wide range of locations. Some are open on weekends as well as in the evenings. This is an excellent option for people who work full-time and are unable to get to a post office branch.

Many of these locations are part of popular retail chains such as Walmart and Costco. This allows you to do some shopping at the same time as sending your parcel. Some also offer other postal services for the convenience of customers.
